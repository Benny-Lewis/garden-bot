# Red Flowering Currant (*Ribes sanguineum*)

- Role: Fence screening
- Location: Side yard — south entry, along east fence
- Native range: PNW, BC to California
- Size: 6-8' tall, 5-6' wide
- Sun: Full sun to part shade
- Water: Low once established
- Soil: Well-drained, tolerates clay
- Bloom: Pink to deep rose, February-March
- Wildlife: Major early-season hummingbird food source, berries for birds
- Notes: One of the first PNW shrubs to bloom — provides late-winter color when the garden is mostly dormant. Blue-black berries follow.
