# Oregon Grape

- Species: *Mahonia aquifolium*
- Type: Evergreen shrub
- Location: Front yard, left bed near sidewalk
- Qty: 2
- Zone: 8b
- Sun: Full sun to part shade
- Mature size: 3–6 ft tall, 3–5 ft wide
- Bloom: Yellow clusters, early spring
- Fruit: Blue-purple tart berries, summer (edible, best in jelly)
- Native: Yes (PNW) — Oregon state flower
- Notes: Holly-like evergreen leaves provide year-round structure. Tolerates dry shade once established. Berries are tart raw but make excellent jelly.
