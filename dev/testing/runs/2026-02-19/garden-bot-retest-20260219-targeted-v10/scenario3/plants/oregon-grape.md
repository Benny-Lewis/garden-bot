# Oregon Grape

- Botanical: Mahonia aquifolium
- Type: Evergreen native shrub
- Zone: 5–9
- Size: 3–6 ft tall, 3–5 ft wide
- Area: Front yard — right accent bed
- Role: PNW native, stealth edible berries
- Sun: Full sun to part shade
- Water: Low once established
- Notes: Oregon state flower. Yellow spring blooms, blue-purple edible berries in summer (tart, good for jelly). Holly-like evergreen foliage turns burgundy in winter. Extremely low maintenance.
