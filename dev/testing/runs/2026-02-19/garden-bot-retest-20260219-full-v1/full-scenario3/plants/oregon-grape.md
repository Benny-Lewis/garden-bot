# Oregon Grape (Low)
- Botanical: Mahonia aquifolium var. compacta (or M. nervosa)
- Type: Evergreen shrub
- Area: Front yard — right gravel garden
- Size: 2-3 ft tall, 2-3 ft wide
- Sun: Full sun to shade
- Water: Low once established
- Edible: Yes — tart blue berries (good for jelly)
- Native: PNW native (Oregon state flower)
- Notes: Holly-like evergreen foliage turns bronze-purple in winter. Yellow flower clusters in spring. Extremely tough and adaptable. Low variety stays compact in the gravel garden.
