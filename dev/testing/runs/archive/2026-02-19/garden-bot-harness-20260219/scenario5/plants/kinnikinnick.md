# Kinnikinnick (*Arctostaphylos uva-ursi*)

- Role: Evergreen groundcover
- Location: Side yard — all zones, between ferns and stepping stones
- Native range: Circumboreal, common in PNW
- Size: 6" tall, spreading mat to 6'+ wide
- Sun: Full sun to part shade
- Water: Very low once established
- Soil: Well-drained, tolerates poor/sandy soil
- Evergreen: Yes — small glossy leaves
- Bloom: Pink-white bell flowers, spring
- Berries: Bright red, fall-winter
- Notes: Creates the moss-like groundplane characteristic of Japanese gardens, but with more structure. Pairs with native moss in shadier spots under the vine maple.
