# Low Oregon Grape (*Mahonia nervosa*)

- Role: Low evergreen structure
- Location: Side yard — south entry
- Native range: PNW forests, BC to California
- Size: 2-3' tall, spreading
- Sun: Shade to part shade (tolerates deep shade)
- Water: Very low once established
- Soil: Adaptable
- Evergreen: Yes — holly-like leaves
- Bloom: Yellow clusters, March-April
- Berries: Blue-purple, edible (tart), summer
- Wildlife: Early pollinator food, berries for birds
- Notes: Oregon's state flower. Provides year-round structure with textural, holly-like foliage. Spreads slowly by rhizome.
